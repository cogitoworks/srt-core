## 0. Purpose

This specification defines the operational conditions for preventing collapse into impression-criticism, ranking-criticism, or the theorization of likes and dislikes when SRT is applied by external LLMs or third parties using only SRT vocabulary.

"External-application collapse" here refers to a state in which vocabulary such as Core / Border / Outer, excessive reattribution, community-proliferation layer, and platform-amplification layer is being used, yet SRT's auditing procedures are in fact not activated, or have converted midway into a different kind of output.

The concern of this specification is not the simplification of SRT. It is the establishment of a boundary that prevents sloppy criticism using SRT vocabulary from being counted as valid SRT application.

---

## 1. Problem Definition

SRT is not a theory for pronouncing the presence or absence of value; it is a theory for auditing the confusion between value-origin and attribution-target.

However, in external operation, the following kinds of collapse frequently occur:

- The value-claim to be audited is not fixated.
- Primary-object anchor verification is skipped.
- Origination-layer strength and accounting categories are short-circuited.
- Portability and substitutability are misused as impressionistic terms.
- NOT-boundaries are treated as supplementary cautionary notes.
- Existing aesthetic judgments or genre hierarchies are repackaged in SRT vocabulary.

This state is not SRT application. It is a different kind of criticism that uses SRT vocabulary.

Therefore, this specification asks—prior to what an external output has judged via SRT—whether SRT was activated in the first place, and whether it mutated into something else midway.

---

## 2. Fixed Points

This specification establishes the following as fixed points.

First, SRT is not a blanket-rating theory for entire works or entire objects. SRT's auditing unit is not the object itself but the individual value-claim submitted about that object.

Second, SRT is not a theory for judging the presence or absence of value. What SRT asks is not whether value exists, but where that value should be credited.

Third, origination layers and accounting categories are not identical. The fact that the community-proliferation layer or platform-amplification layer is strong does not by itself mean Outer is confirmed.

Fourth, NOT is not a supplementary explanation. NOT is not an atmospheric description of the theory but an operational boundary for determining mutation when boundaries are crossed.

Fifth, the use of SRT vocabulary is not a condition for valid SRT application. Even if terms such as Core / Border / Outer appear, the application is considered invalid if procedures are missing.

---

## 3. State Categories

External output is distinguished into at least the following three states.

### 3.1 SRT-Not-Activated

A state in which SRT vocabulary appears but procedures such as audit-claim fixation, primary-object anchor requirements, reconnection verification, and alternative-explanation comparison are not operating.

Here, vocabulary is merely borrowed; the auditing procedure itself has not been initiated.

### 3.2 SRT-Mis-Activated

A state in which procedures have partially initiated but identity as an auditing theory has been lost midway through short-circuiting of accounting categories, failure to inspect NOT, or instant verdicts through mere enumeration of external factors.

Here, activation has occurred, but mutation into something else has progressed during operation.

### 3.3 SRT-Application-Failed

A state in which procedures themselves have been correctly activated, but provisional Core / Border / Outer judgment is unstable due to insufficient materials, insufficient anchors, unresolved alternative explanations, or excessive dispute points.

This differs from not-activated and mis-activated. Application failure is deferral or judgment-inability premised on the procedures having been validly constituted.

## 4. Pre-Application Gates

When external LLMs or third parties apply SRT, the following pre-gates are mandatory. If even one is unfulfilled, the output is not considered valid SRT application.

### Gate-1: Audit-Claim Fixation

First, fixate in a single sentence the value-claim to be audited.

Examples:

"This work is a sharp critique of contemporary society."

"The emotional impact of this work is largely supported by the text."

"This work is a literary masterpiece."

The entire work or entire object must not be made the judgment target in bulk.

### Gate-2: Primary-Object Anchor Requirement

For the fixated claim, enumerate concrete elements that return to the primary support base of the auditing target itself.

For novels, this includes the body text. For visual works, the screen, composition, dialogue, and direction. For games, the implementation, UI, progression, and systems. For theoretical texts, the definitions, argumentation connections, and exclusion clauses. These are what is included here.

Claims that cannot return to the primary object are placed on credit-deferral at that point.

### Gate-3: Reconnection Verification

Verify that the enumerated elements genuinely connect to the claim.

"That element exists" and "that value is the work of the object itself" are not identical. One must not proceed to credit-to-object merely by confirming element existence.

### Gate-4: Alternative-Explanation Comparison

Compare whether the same value-claim holds equally well or better through explanations other than the object itself.

At minimum, the following candidates must be included in the comparison:

- Community interpretation-proliferation
- Pre-reading expectations / prior context
- Institutional authority-conferral
- Platform amplification
- Genre-convention supplementation
- Generality of recipient-side supplementation

### Gate-5: Accounting-Category Judgment

Only after the above procedures may a provisional Core / Border / Outer judgment be given.

The order must not be reversed. An operation that places accounting categories first and then collects justifications violates SRT procedure.

### Gate-6: Open Checks Declaration

Unverified matters, deferral points, judgment-inability points, and residual dispute points must always be left.

The output must not close with a sealed verdict.

## 5. Invalid-Application Criteria

Any output matching any of the following is invalid as SRT application.

① Placing an entire work or entire object in Core / Border / Outer in bulk without fixating an audit-claim.

② Short-circuiting to conclusions such as "community-proliferation layer is strong, therefore Outer" or "topicality is high, therefore Outer" without primary-object anchors.

③ Pronouncing excessive reattribution merely by citing BookTok, award history, social-media topicality, credentials, or buzzwords.

④ Treating portability and substitutability as mere impressionistic paraphrasability.

⑤ Repackaging genre hierarchies, personal likes and dislikes, existing evaluations, or cultural position-taking directly as SRT judgments.

⑥ Directly connecting aesthetic terms such as "deep," "shallow," "ordinary," or "thin" to accounting categories while skipping primary-object anchor verification and alternative-explanation comparison.

⑦ Mass-producing application examples or judgment examples without conducting NOT-violation inspection.

⑧ Closing with an instant verdict without producing Open Checks.

These are not invalidations based on the favorability of the conclusion's content. They are invalidations in the sense that the activation conditions for an auditing theory have not been met, or that collapse has occurred midway.

---

## 6. NOT-Boundary Operational Re-Fixation

In this specification, the following are re-fixated as NOT-boundaries for SRT external operation.

SRT is not a work-ranking apparatus.

SRT is not a genre-hierarchy justification apparatus.

SRT is not a theory for devaluing buzz-driven works.

SRT is not a relabeling of highbrow/lowbrow.

SRT is not the theorization of impression-criticism into theory-vocabulary.

SRT is not a shortcut for assigning Outer merely by observing BookTok or social-media trends.

SRT is not a shortcut for presenting accounting categories while skipping primary-object reading.

SRT is not a vocabulary set for disguising personal preference as auditing results.

The NOT here is not negation-rhetoric but an operational boundary for preserving theoretical identity.

---

## 7. Output Constraints for External LLMs

When SRT is applied through external LLMs, free-form writing first is prohibited.

The reason is that natural-language fluency alone can make an output appear as if auditing has been validly constituted, even when procedures have not been activated.

Therefore, in external application, the fixed format must be fulfilled first, and only afterward may general assessment or critical-essay-style writing be permitted.

---

## 8. Fixed Auditing Format

External LLMs or third parties must, when applying SRT, fill in at minimum the following format first.

**[SRT Provisional Auditing Format]**

1. Audit-target claim
2. Primary-object anchor candidates
3. Reconnectability to claim (yes / partial / no)
4. Alternative-explanation candidates
5. Origination-layer memo
6. Core / Border / Outer provisional judgment
7. NOT-violation check
8. Open Checks

Free-form general assessment is permitted only after this format has been fulfilled.

The purpose is to force auditing-procedure activation ahead of natural-language fluency.

---

## 9. Relationship to the Core Text

This specification is not a replacement for the SRT Core 0.7 text.

What the core text bears is: the definition of value-attribution auditing theory, origination layers, accounting categories, the four axes, epistemological position, NOT, and self-auditability.

What this specification bears is: the validity conditions, invalid-application criteria, and operational constraints for those elements to activate without mutating during external operation.

Therefore, when SRT is handed to an external party, the core text alone must not be handed over. At minimum, the core text and this specification must be presented as a pair.

---

## 10. Open Checks

The major issues remaining at this point are as follows.

First, over-proliferation of pre-gates may increase operational burden and reduce practical usability. What constitutes mandatory activation conditions versus precision-auditing conditions has not been fixed.

Second, the granularity of primary-object anchors—how much specificity to require—has not been fixed. Media-specific and object-specific concreteness standards are needed.

Third, further definitional precision for portability and substitutability is needed. In external operation, the two are frequently conflated.

Fourth, in what format NOT-boundaries should be provided to external LLMs in order to function most effectively as boundaries has not been verified.

Fifth, how far the distinction among SRT-not-activated, SRT-mis-activated, and SRT-application-failed can be stably operated requires the accumulation of operational examples.

Sixth, if the fixed format becomes overly rigid, there is a risk of degradation from auditing support into a form-filling ritual. A balance between operability and boundary-preservation is needed.

---

## 11. Closing

SRT's problem is not that its theoretical vocabulary is insufficient. Rather, it is the opposite. The fact that output appearing to constitute valid auditing can be generated with vocabulary alone is one cause of external collapse.

Therefore, what is needed when releasing SRT externally is—ahead of more fluent explanation—the explicit codification of what constitutes valid application, what constitutes not-activated, what constitutes mis-activated, and what constitutes invalid.

This specification is the perimeter boundary for that purpose.
