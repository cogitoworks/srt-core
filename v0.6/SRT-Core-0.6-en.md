## 0. Intuition

When people encounter works, agents, theories, or discourse, they often count not only what they received from the object itself but also the richness produced by the recipient's own supplementation, community discussion, and platform-environment amplification—all as "the object's intrinsic achievement."

What happens here is not mere misreading. Meaning and value often genuinely increase. The problem is not the increased value itself but rather that it becomes unclear whose work that increased value should be credited to.

Semantic Reattribution Theory (hereafter SRT) is a framework for auditing this ambiguity. SRT's concern is not "what can be read" as such, nor "how much excitement was generated" or "how deep it appears" as such.

What SRT audits is not the presence or absence of value, but the confusion between where value originates and to whom it is attributed.

Accordingly, SRT's core proposition can be summarized as follows:

**That something has value and whose work that value should be credited to are separate questions.**

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Existing reception theories and interpretation theories have richly described how readers and communities actively participate in meaning-making. Their contributions are significant. However, when value and richness generated through the reception process come to be recounted as the intrinsic achievement of the object itself, a separate auditing perspective becomes necessary.

For example, suppose rich readings of a work proliferate within a community. That richness itself should not be denied. But when the question of where that richness primarily arose and how much of it may be credited to the work itself is left undistinguished, and the work is described as "this deep," the accounting between value-origin and credit-attribution becomes confused.

The same can occur not only with works but also with persons, theories, discourse, brands, articles, and topics on platforms. When credentials, recommendation pathways, community memes, and algorithmic exposure are added, value exceeding the object's own work is advanced on credit and becomes more readily read as the object's own achievement.

SRT treats such confusion as a problem of "value-attribution auditing."

---

## 2. Core Definition of SRT

SRT is an operationally grounded value-attribution auditing framework that audits the structure by which meaning, value, and richness generated or amplified through the processes of reception, community, and platform come to be recounted as the intrinsic achievement of works, agents, or theories themselves.

What is important here is that SRT is not a theory that denies value itself. SRT does not immediately pronounce externally generated value as false. Value generated through community proliferation or platform amplification often possesses genuine cultural reality.

The problem is that the origin point of that value is forgotten, and it is recounted as-is as the object's intrinsic achievement.

Therefore, SRT's purpose is neither to determine the single correct reading nor to constrain the freedom of meaning-making, but to make visible—in an auditable form—the confusion between value-origin and attribution-target.

---

## 3. The Origination-Layer Model

SRT employs the following four layers to distinguish where value originates.

### 3.1 Work-Immanent Layer

Value origination supported by the object's own form, arrangement, description, and structure. Here, value is supported in a manner reconnectable to concrete elements within the object.

### 3.2 Elicited-Reception Layer

Value origination that relies on internal support from the object while proliferating through the recipient's supplementation, inference, association, and empathy. This is not entirely independent of the object, but it contains richness that would not exist without the recipient's participation.

### 3.3 Community-Proliferation Layer

Value origination that proliferates on the community side—in fandoms, critical communities, social media, meme spheres, fan-creation spheres, and so on—using the object as a foothold. Here, interpretations, emotions, and conventions can self-proliferate within the community.

### 3.4 Platform-Amplification Layer

Value origination that is externally advanced on credit or amplified through UI, recommendation pathways, credential display, controversy, topicality, rankings, algorithmic exposure, authority-conferral, and similar mechanisms. Pre-reading expectation-value is often formed here in advance.

What is important is that these four layers are categories that ask "where did it originate?" They are not categories that directly determine how much may be credited to the object itself.

---

## 3-b. Temporal Axis and Attribution Fluctuation

The value-attributions that SRT addresses are not statically fixed. The relationship between value-origin and credit-to-object can fluctuate through processes of reception, community generation, platform amplification, re-reading, re-evaluation, and repositioning.

Therefore, SRT's origination-layer model and accounting categories are not classifications that finalize the object's essence in a single pass. They are auditing categories for describing the value-origin and credit-state observed at a given point in time.

Consequently, even the same value may be placed in different categories depending on the time point. For example, value that initially depended heavily on community proliferation or platform amplification and was placed in Border or Outer may later be repositioned toward Core through reconnection with internal support from the object. Conversely, value that initially relied strongly on internal support may later come to depend heavily on community-induced convergence through conventionalization or memification, and may be re-evaluated toward Border or Outer.

Therefore, auditing in SRT does not mean finalizing value in a single judgment. It includes tracking and describing when a given value proliferated, in which layer, with what kind of support, and to what extent it was credited to the object at each point in time.

In this sense, SRT's categories are not fixed essences but time-dependent auditing descriptions.

---

## 4. Accounting Categories: Core / Border / Outer

SRT employs the following three categories to assess how much of the generated value may be credited to the object itself.

### 4.1 Core

The domain where internal support from the object is strong and the value can be primarily credited as the object's intrinsic achievement.

### 4.2 Border

A mixed-evaluation domain where the value relies to some degree on internal support from the object while also containing significant dependence on community proliferation or platform amplification, and therefore should be limited to partial crediting as the object's intrinsic achievement.

Border is not merely a vague middle ground. Rather, it is an active domain for partial crediting, credit-deferral, and dispute-point visualization. Where necessary, it can be further subdivided into Core-leaning Border and Outer-leaning Border.

### 4.3 Outer

The domain where the central origination of value lies outside the object, and the value should not be credited as the object's intrinsic achievement, or should be strongly deferred.

What is important here is that the origination-layer model and the accounting categories are separate concepts. The former asks about value-origin. The latter asks about the permissibility and degree of crediting to the object.

---

## 5. Permissible Reattribution and Excessive Reattribution

SRT does not treat value proliferation uniformly. To clarify the difference in accounting treatment, it distinguishes between permissible reattribution and excessive reattribution.

### 5.1 Permissible Reattribution

Partially recrediting to the object value proliferation that relies considerably on internal support from the object. Here, even though reception and community participation are involved, there is sufficient internal support to justify returning a certain degree of credit to the object's own work.

### 5.2 Excessive Reattribution

Recrediting to the object value that is primarily externally generated, as if it were the object's intrinsic achievement. Here, the work of the community or platform is being over-accounted as the object's intrinsic achievement.

The key point of this distinction is not to deny the existence of value proliferation itself. The problem is how much of that increased value may be credited as the object's own work.

---

## 6. Four Axes for Judgment

SRT does not leave credit-to-object judgments to a single impression but audits them through at least the following four axes.

### 6.1 Internal Anchor

This axis asks whether the claim or value judgment can be reconnected to concrete elements within the object. It concerns where a reading or evaluation can be traced back to for verification.

### 6.2 Convergence

This axis asks to what degree multiple recipients can reconstruct their readings at similar positions. However, convergence is not a single concept. SRT bifurcates it.

#### 6.2.1 Base-Layer Convergence

Convergence that arises through reconnection to internal features of the object.

#### 6.2.2 Community-Induced Convergence

Convergence formed externally through memes, stock phrases, existing reputation, shared templates, ambient discourse, and similar mechanisms.

Convergence judgment should not be based solely on present agreement. Where possible, it should be examined from the perspective of temporal history—whether the convergence was observable before community conventionalization or was formed as a subsequent induced convergence.

### 6.3 Portability

This axis asks whether the value can be easily re-applied to a different object. The higher the re-applicability, the stronger the suspicion that the value is not the object's specific work but an externally circulating value pattern.

### 6.4 Substitutability

This axis asks whether the value judgment requires the object's specific structure or whether an alternative explanation would equally hold. If an alternative explanation readily holds, strong crediting to the object should be treated with caution.

These four axes are not indicators for mechanically producing conclusions on their own. In SRT, they serve as procedural scaffolding for auditing Core crediting, Border deferral, and Outer suspicion.

---

## 6-1. General Form of Internal-Anchor Judgment

An internal anchor in SRT is an auditing scaffold that asks whether a given claim or value judgment can be reconnected to referenceable elements within the object.

"Internal" here does not mean metaphysical essence. It refers to where, within the object's composition, description, presentation, or behavior—as opposed to externally attached value—one can return to verify a judgment about the object.

Therefore, the target of internal-anchor judgment is not limited to the text of a work. Depending on the type of object, the reference points may differ as follows:

For works: body text, depiction, composition, dialogue, visual framing, direction, rules, systems, and so on.

For theories: definition statements, conceptual arrangements, argumentation procedures, usage examples, exclusion clauses, and so on.

For thinkers or commentators: public utterances, repeated descriptions, self-positioning in discussion, consistency of responses, and so on.

For AI personalities or dialogue agents: sustained response tendencies, constraint conditions, repeated behaviors, explicit rules, and so on.

For brands or personal evaluations: primary statements, behavioral records, presentation styles, sustained practices, and so on.

What is important is that although SRT is applicable to multiple object types, it must not be applied across objects without first defining the primary support base for each. For any object, one must first establish what constitutes the primary support base for that object, and only then audit whether claims or value judgments can be reconnected to it.

Therefore, the internal anchor is not a technique limited to a specific medium but a general auditing procedure operated after defining the primary support base for each object.

## 6-1a. Text-Anchor Judgment Procedure

Text-anchor judgment is a specialized form of internal-anchor judgment, applied to objects where one can return to "the text"—such as a work's text or compositional structure.

Text-anchor judgment is not a procedure that adopts LLM or reader impression-evaluations as-is. It is an auditing procedure that asks whether a claim or value judgment can be reconnected to concrete elements within the object.

The procedure consists of the following five stages.

### 6-1a-1. Claim Extraction

Make explicit what is being claimed.

### 6-1a-2. Request for Textual Evidence

Request that the concrete internal elements supporting the claim be indicated.

### 6-1a-3. Reconnectability Verification

Verify whether one can re-arrive at the claim from the indicated elements.

The minimum conditions for reconnection to hold are two.

First, the indicated internal elements of the object must be independently verifiable without first accepting the claim in question.

Second, the pathway from those elements to the claim must be describable without presupposing the claim itself.

Conversely, if the indicated elements cannot be verified without presupposing the claim, or if the pathway from elements to claim cannot be written without presupposing the claim, then reconnection does not hold.

However, what these conditions control extends only to element-verifiability and pathway non-circularity.

How strongly those elements support the claim—the recognition of connection-strength—still involves interpretive judgment.

This remainder corresponds to what §8 calls residual circularity.

### 6-1a-4. Comparison with Alternative Explanations

Verify whether an alternative explanation holds equally well or better from the same elements.

### 6-1a-5. Context-Removal Test

Verify whether internal support from the object remains when community conventions and surrounding knowledge are removed.

In this procedure, LLMs may be used as aids for candidate extraction and comparative explanation. However, they are not the final decision-makers of anchor existence. Final judgment is borne by humans within the overall auditing procedure.

What is important here is that text-anchor judgment is not a procedure completed merely by "confirming the existence of features." Even if referenceable features within the object exist, recognizing how strongly those features support the claim in question still involves interpretive judgment.

In this sense, text-anchor judgment is not a procedure that automatically confirms claim-evidence connections without interpretation. It is a procedure that separates what can be controlled through verifiability and non-circularity conditions from where connection-strength judgment remains, repositioning residual circularity into an auditable form.

## 6-b. Judgment Discretion, Deferral, and Reproducibility

SRT is not a theory that numerically auto-determines value-attribution judgments. Nor is it a theory that directly legitimizes arbitrary impressions or one-off reactions as discretion.

What SRT addresses is the confusion between value-origin and attribution-target. And this auditing inevitably involves context-dependent judgment. Therefore, SRT is positioned not as a mechanical scoring theory but as an auditing-procedure theory.

What is important here is that over-reducing judgments to numerical values risks inverting the theory's purpose from auditing to metric-optimization. For example, if single indicators such as text-anchor scores, Border indices, or Outer contamination rates become foregrounded, pseudo-depth and audit-evasive descriptions adapted to those indicators may proliferate. This would produce not the improvement of value-attribution auditing but the mass production of depth-seemingness optimized for auditing criteria.

Therefore, SRT's purpose is not the automation of evaluation. Rather, it is to restrain value-attribution judgments from flowing into premature automation, enabling deferral, partial crediting, and dispute-point sharing.

In this sense, SRT is not an apparatus for instant final verdicts. It is an auditing apparatus for detecting suspicion, separating dispute points, and slowing down Core crediting.

Reproducibility in SRT does not mean that the same conclusion or the same score is always produced for the same case. The minimum conditions SRT requires are three.

First, re-describability of judgment rationale. It must be possible to re-articulate why a judgment was reached, using the four axes, origination layers, and accounting categories.

Second, shareability of dispute-point location. It must be possible to show, in a comparable form, at which axis, which connection, or which layer-boundary the judgment diverges.

Third, presentability of deferral rationale. It must be possible to make explicit why Core crediting is withheld, why something is kept in Border, or why Outer suspicion remains.

Therefore, SRT's output is more appropriately described as layer-classifications, flags, deferral-states, and dispute-memos than as a single score. For example, descriptions such as "text anchor weak," "community-induced convergence strong," "authority advance-credit present," "platform attachment present," "Core crediting deferred," and "Border residence recommended" are auditing memos, not final verdicts.

Through such auditing memos, SRT does not instantly deny value but rather separates its origin point and crediting conditions, maintains undetermined states, and secures comparability.

Hence, SRT is a theory that supports the auditing of value-attribution, not a theory that automates final judgment through mechanical scoring.

## 7. Margin and Void

In SRT, margin and void are concepts that should be distinguished.

A margin is a domain where the object structurally supports and elicits supplementation. There, the recipient's participation is necessary, but that participation is supported by internal support from the object.

A void is a gap left with weak support. There, recipients and communities can freely add richness, but that richness is not necessarily strongly supported by the object itself.

Although the two may appear similar, they differ. Margins can serve as footholds for permissible reattribution, while voids tend to become breeding grounds for excessive reattribution.

Therefore, the mere fact that "readings increased" does not determine whether they derive from margin or void. Reconnectability to internal support from the object is required.

## 8. Epistemological Position

SRT does not fix internal meaning-structure as strong realism. Nor does it dissolve everything into reception as complete constructivism.

What SRT employs as its auditing scaffold is the bundle of formal, compositional, and descriptive features within the object that multiple recipients can reference. The axis emphasized here is not "what it ultimately means" but "where one can return to verify."

Therefore, the internal-support structure in SRT is defined not as a claim of metaphysical essence but as an operational base for auditing procedures.

However, introducing referenceability does not make circularity disappear entirely. Recognizing which features support which claims to what degree still involves interpretive judgment. What remains here is not circularity in the sense that internal features of the object cannot be verified, but residual circularity at the level of how to recognize the bridge between verified features and claims.

SRT does not conceal this residual circularity as a theoretical failure. Nor does it treat referenceability as if it alone could enable completely neutral judgment. Rather, SRT acknowledges that this level of interpretive judgment is unavoidable in value-attribution auditing, and adopts the stance of preventing that unavoidability from flowing into unconstrained impression-criticism—controlling it through auditing procedures, dispute-point sharing, deferral judgments, and alternative-explanation comparison.

Therefore, epistemological modesty in SRT does not mean declaring the complete removal of circularity. Rather, it means distinguishing what constitutes referenceable scaffolding from what constitutes connection-recognition that can be disputed, and treating the remainder self-consciously.

In this sense, SRT is not a theory that claims the absence of circularity, but a theory that decomposes residual circularity into auditable units, making their position and strength visible during operation.

## 9. Application Targets and Practical Implications

SRT is applicable to a wide range of objects: literary works, visual works, games, characters, criticism, thought, articles, personal evaluations, brand evaluations, discourse on social media, and more. However, its purpose is not to deliver a sweeping judgment on the object's value.

SRT's practical significance includes at least two dimensions.

First, post-reception value-attribution auditing. That is, auditing how much of the value and richness that arose around an object may be credited as the object's own achievement, from the perspectives of both origination layers and accounting categories.

Second, pre-reception expectation-value auditing. When expectation-value is advanced on credit before reading through credentials, UI, recommendations, topicality, community enthusiasm, and so on, auditing where that expectation comes from.

In this respect, SRT can be applied not only to post-reception evaluation correction but also to pre-reception value-advance auditing.

That said, its generality is not unconditional. When applying SRT to multiple object types, one must first define what constitutes the primary support base for each object. Therefore, SRT's generality is not unconditional cross-applicability but conditional generality premised on object-specific support-base definition.

Furthermore, its operation should, where possible, be conducted in a time-dependent manner. Tracking when a given value began to depend on community proliferation, or at what point its reconnection with internal support strengthened, leads to more precise auditing than static judgment.

## 10. NOT-Boundaries

SRT is not any of the following kinds of theory.

SRT is not a theory of simple return to authorial intent.

SRT is not a wholesale denial of reader freedom.

SRT is not a theory for determining the single correct reading.

SRT is not a theory for invalidating external value.

SRT is not a theory that immediately pronounces buzz or community enthusiasm as false.

SRT is not a theory whose purpose is deep-reading criticism itself.

SRT is not a theory that reduces value evaluation to a single numerical score.

SRT is not a design theory for audit-evasion games that incentivize optimization toward judgment metrics.

SRT is not a theory that forces immediate final verdicts.

SRT is not a theory that may be applied across object types without defining the primary support base for each, merely because it is applicable to multiple objects.

SRT is not a theory that may unlimitedly recover externally attached value as "internal" under the name of referenceability.

SRT is not a theory that may fix a single point-in-time observation as a permanent essence.

To supplement: what SRT audits is not the presence or absence of value but the confusion between value-origin and attribution-target. SRT is an auditing-procedure theory that permits deferral, partial crediting, and dispute-point sharing.

---

## 11. Self-Auditability

SRT is not merely a theory for auditing others' value-attributions. SRT itself can also be returned to the same auditing target.

When a theory spreads, acquires a name, gains a supporting community, is cited, and takes on authority, SRT itself can also be externally endowed with richness beyond its substance. At that point, SRT can become a target of the very reattribution it was supposed to audit.

Therefore, SRT must not fix itself as the final authority on value-attribution judgment. What SRT needs is not the sanctification of its own incompleteness, but self-auditability—the capacity to return its own boundary-setting, judgment procedures, and authority-acquisition processes back to the auditing target.

In this sense, SRT's openness is not decorative. It is a functional condition for controlling the risk that the theory itself becomes a target of reattribution.

## 12. Current Status

Given the above, the most accurate self-definition of SRT at the current stage is the following expression:

**SRT is an operationally grounded value-attribution auditing framework.**

Expanded further:

**SRT is an operationally grounded value-attribution auditing framework that audits the structure by which value generated or amplified through the processes of reception, community, and platform comes to be recounted as the intrinsic achievement of works, agents, or theories themselves.**

This is stronger than a mere conceptual memo. But at the same time, it is not a completed judgment theory or rigorous numerical model. It is appropriate to understand SRT at this stage as an auditing framework equipped with a theoretical core and the skeleton of operational procedures.

## 13. Open Checks

The major issues remaining at this point are as follows.

First, controlling threshold judgments. How to control terms such as "relies considerably," "contains significantly," and "to some degree"—not through quantification but as conditions for auditability—is an important future task.

Second, organizing judgment discretion. The question is how far discretion can be organized as re-describability, dispute-point shareability, and deferral-rationale presentability without allowing it to become arbitrary.

Third, handling boundary cases between base-layer convergence and community-induced convergence. How to organize cases where both are intertwined requires the accumulation of concrete examples. In particular, how far initial convergence and subsequent induced convergence can be distinguished relates to the precision of time-axis-inclusive auditing description.

Fourth, improving judgment precision for margin and void. The connection with text-anchor procedures needs further refinement.

Fifth, developing pre-reception expectation-value auditing. How to handle authority advance-crediting, UI attachment, and topicality foregrounding before reading—not just post-reception auditing—is an extension area for SRT.

Sixth, sub-differentiation of Border. Under what conditions Core-leaning Border and Outer-leaning Border can be stably introduced remains an open issue.

Seventh, establishing a standard format for audit-memo-type output. How far to formalize must be determined while balancing operability against rigidification risk.

Eighth, the problem of where to position the equilibrium between theoretical consistency and operational viability, given that residual circularity is presupposed, remains.

Ninth, operationalizing self-auditability. How far SRT's own authority-acquisition, community-formation, and banner-ification can be internally audited—and at what point they generate reattribution—is an important future task.

Tenth, methods for defining primary support bases in multi-object application. How far object-specific support-base definitions can be standardized relates to both SRT's generality and its precision.

Eleventh, at what granularity to describe temporal fluctuations in value-origin and accounting categories. How to incorporate attribution fluctuations caused by revised editions, derivative works, critical circulation, algorithm changes, and similar factors into SRT's time-series description remains unresolved.

---

## 14. Closing

SRT is not a theory hostile to value proliferation. The fact that value increases through the workings of reception, community, and platform is often realistic and productive as a cultural phenomenon.

However, if whose work that value should be credited to remains ambiguous, evaluations of works, agents, theories, and discourse easily become confused.

SRT's purpose is to audit this confusion. More precisely, it is to slow down premature crediting to the object, to separate value-origin from attribution-target, and to enable deferral and partial crediting.

In this sense, SRT is not a theory that rushes to final verdict. Rather, it is an auditing framework for resisting the automation of evaluation and re-asking where value was born and how far it may be credited back.
